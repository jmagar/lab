# Subscribe to our terms and conditions
Tailscale updated our terms and conditions today. Specifically, we updated our [Terms of Service](/terms/) and our [Privacy Policy](/privacy-policy/).
We can’t summarize the changes, because, well, we’re not lawyers, and lawyers like it when we link to the full language of the change — apparently if you *don’t* talk in legalese, it’s not legal!
We are, however, developers. Developers are familiar with diffs. So, what if we just gave you a diff of the terms? (It’s way better than trying to piece it together yourself using the Internet Archive’s Wayback Machine.) We already [use git](https://github.com/tailscale/security-policies) to track changes to our [internal security policies](/security-policies). So, we’ve done the same here — our legal terms and conditions now have their source of truth on GitHub, in the [tailscale/terms-and-conditions](https://github.com/tailscale/terms-and-conditions) repo.
In addition to allowing you to easily see a particular diff ([like today’s](https://github.com/tailscale/terms-and-conditions/pull/3/files)), putting our terms on GitHub means you can see how they change over time. And, you can watch the repo to be notified of any new or upcoming changes. (h/t to [GitHub for inspiration](https://github.com/github/site-policy))
If you’re not already aware of all of our terms, policies, and various other compliance docs, check out our [Legal page](/legal). Like and subscribe to our [terms](https://github.com/tailscale/terms-and-conditions)!
