# A network of pangolins
Tailscale is named as such because we want to allow [small, human-scale teams to build trusted networks](/about) easily — without having the complexity or long *tail* of software and operational problems, which don’t *scale* well to a small team. Tail. Scale.
Sure, sure, that’s a good vision[1](#one). Anyways, do you know [there are a *lot* of things with either tails or scales](https://github.com/tailscale/tailscale/tree/main/words)? And a lot of them are animals!
[Pangolins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pangolin) have both tails and scales, so they are obviously the unofficial mascot of Tailscale. (And not just any kind of tail or scale — a prehensile tail, and scales made of keratin!) We think pangolins are super cute. And they’re nocturnal, just like us!
You know how there are weird words for groups of animals, like a murder of crows, or a parliament of owls? There is no official collective noun for pangolins, because they’re solitary creatures.
Although Tailscale is a remote company, [Tailscalars do occasionally meet up](/blog/trainscale/). We wanted a way to refer to our unofficial mascot. So, we’re proposing a collective noun for pangolins[2](#two): *a network of pangolins*[3](#one). Tell our Marketing team we’re going to have great SEO!
Small, trusted, ~~human-~~ mammalian-scale networks. Who says we have to innovate only in networking[4](#two) and can’t also innovate in the English language[5](#five)?
1Unlike pangolins, who have poor vision. But by reading this footnote you’ve skipped ahead to the animal reveal, oh no!
2We can’t use *vector*, which is the collective noun for a group of Tailscalars — they’re *pangolins* not *pangolines*.
3The other option was *a cURL of pangolins*, because they curl up! So cute.
4See [how NAT traversal works](/blog/how-nat-traversal-works/) in Tailscale, or [a look at MagicDNS behind the scenes](/blog/magicdns-why-name/).
5At least, [that’s what we learned](https://www.andrewclements.com/books/frindle) in school.
